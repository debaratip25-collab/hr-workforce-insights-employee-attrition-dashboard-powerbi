# HR Workforce Insights & Employee Attrition Dashboard | Power BI

## 📌 Project Overview

This project is a professional HR analytics dashboard built in Power BI to analyze workforce composition, employee attrition trends, department performance, compensation patterns, and retention risk indicators.

The solution uses a star-schema data model with multiple related datasets and DAX measures to deliver interactive, decision-ready insights for HR teams and management.

---

## 🎯 Business Objectives

- Track employee attrition across departments
- Identify workforce retention hotspots
- Compare department headcount, exits, and salary levels
- Analyze the relationship between income, satisfaction, and attrition risk
- Evaluate overtime impact on employee exits
- Support data-driven HR decision-making

---

## 📊 Dashboard Features

### KPI Cards
- Total Employees
- Attrition Count
- Attrition Rate %
- Average Income
- Average Satisfaction Score
- Department with Highest Attrition

### Visualizations
- Attrition by Department (Bar Chart)
- Hiring Trend by Join Year (Line Chart)
- Attrition Hotspot Heatmap (Department × Tenure Band)
- Overtime vs Attrition % (100% Stacked Bar Chart)
- Workforce Retention Risk Map (Scatter Plot)
- Department Summary Matrix
- Interactive Slicers

---

## 🧮 Key DAX Measures

- Total Employees
- Attrition Count

---

## 🗂 Data Model

Star schema design with the following tables:

- DimEmployees
- FactAttrition
- FactCompensation
- FactPerformance

---

## 🔍 Key Business Insights

- Overall attrition rate reached 48% in the sample workforce.
- Sales and Operations emerged as the highest attrition-risk departments.
- IT had the highest average compensation, yet turnover remained elevated.
- Lower satisfaction scores aligned with higher attrition risk.
- Overtime employees showed higher attrition exposure, indicating possible workload pressure.
- HR and Finance demonstrated comparatively stable retention levels.
